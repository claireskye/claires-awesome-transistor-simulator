Wait, how is this just two PN-Junctions?

A BJT transistor is physically just two diodes placed back-to-back, sharing a microscopic middle layer (the Base). Normally, back-to-back diodes block current in both directions.

But because the Base is so incredibly thin, when the first junction (Emitter-Base) is opened, it shoots particles across the Base so fast they fall into the second junction's electric field and get violently swept away before they can stop!
